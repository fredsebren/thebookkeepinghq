What waiting costs

Most new owners don't think about their books until the first tax deadline.

Here's what that looks like when it arrives:

  • A year of cleanup runs into the thousands.

    Reconstructing twelve months of records from bank statements, receipts, and memory takes far longer than keeping them current, and you pay for every hour of it.

  • Mixed business and personal accounts double the work.

    Every transaction has to be sorted by hand before anything can be reconciled. Most new owners run everything through one account for the first year without realizing what it costs later.

  • Missing records can't be recovered.

    Receipts you didn't keep, transactions you can't identify, cash you can't account for. That's money you can't deduct.

  • You make decisions blind.

    You can't tell what's actually profitable until someone puts the numbers in order.

None of this happens because anyone did anything wrong. It happens because year one is busy and the books are the thing that waits.
